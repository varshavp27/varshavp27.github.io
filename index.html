Varsha Prabhu
I am a software developer at Tesco and I completed my undergraduate studies from National Institute of Technology Karnataka, Surathkal. I am passionate about developing products which help me make a direct impact on people's lives.
About me

2005

My interest with Computers started back in 5th grade, when QBASIC was taught at school. At the same time, my uncle was fiddling with Visual Basic. With his encouragement, I learn basic HTML and basic Visual Basic and started developing small games.

2009

Fast forward four years, I was learning basic Java at school when I really started getting inclined towards programming. To continue the interest, I took another course in my 11th and 12th grades where I learn C programming and about pointers. Towards the end of this stint, I naturally went for Computer Science and Engineering as my major for my bachelors' degree.

2013

Since then, the journey has been exciting and overwhelming too, at times. From Data structures, Algorithms, Computer architecture, operating systems, networks, databases, distributed databases and software engineering to information security, image processing, machine intelligence, data mining and data warehousing all college courses have opened up different dimensions of learning and thinking in me. Not to forget the math courses like probability and discrete mathematics.

2014

I was fortunate enough to be a part of The Web Enthusiasts' club at college, during my sophomore year. Attending sessions of Web Club and participating in it's activities gave me an insight about the latest happenings in the technology space, and more importantly, encouraged me to start solving questions on spoj, codechef, hackerrank and codeforces.

2015

Being a part of the club, I was motivated to learn python in my free time. Basic knowledge of Python, along with some problem solving helped me get a two month long software engineering internship at Bilent Services Private Limited, to work on their product Kyash. This was when I got the real sense of backend development and what it meant to write production ready code along with other developers. My experience at Bilent and a lot of luck opened doors for me to be a part of CybrHome as a Backend Developer. During this time, I got a real feel of what it is to work at a very early stage startup. The fast changing dynamics, the need to deliver in time, and the need to be dedicated to the work at a personal level transformed my ways of doing things. This was during the course of the semester, and it was a roller coaster ride to be on par with the work and studies.

2016

I interned at Amazon during the following summer for a brief period of two months. For a person who had been at two startups, Amazon was a totally different experience. The clear processes, the number of in-house developer tools, the stringent code reviews and the sheer pace at which so many teams delivered was great to watch and learn from.
